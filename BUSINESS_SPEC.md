# Business Spec: Thai Nigiri Website Rebuild

## Site Purpose
Thai Nigiri is a fusion restaurant in Downtown Sandpoint, ID that combines bold Thai flavors with the delicate artistry of Japanese sushi. The website must communicate the unique dual-cuisine identity, drive online orders, and make it effortless for locals and visitors to find hours, location, and the full menu.

## Target Audience
- Local Sandpoint residents seeking lunch or dinner
- Tourists visiting downtown Sandpoint
- Food enthusiasts drawn to Thai-Japanese fusion
- Mobile-first users looking to order online or check hours quickly

## Differentiators
- Rare Thai + Japanese fusion concept in a small-town market
- House-made sushi rolls alongside authentic Thai curries and ramen
- Warm, welcoming downtown atmosphere
- Consistent quality across both cuisines

## Sitemap
| Route | Priority | Purpose |
|-------|----------|---------|
| `/` | High | Brand intro, featured dishes, order CTA, hours |
| `/menu` | High | Full menu organized by category |
| `/about` | Medium | Story, team, atmosphere |

## Content Types
- **Menu Item** — name, description, price, category, featured flag, image
- **Menu Category** — Sushi Rolls, Thai Entrees, Don Bowls, Ramen, Drinks
- **Pages** — Home, About, Menu (standard editorial pages)

## Brand Guidelines
- **Tone**: Warm, welcoming, authentic, slightly upscale without being pretentious
- **Primary color**: Amber/burnt orange `#C74200` (retained from original brand)
- **Background**: Deep charcoal `#1C1C1C` for hero; warm off-white `#FAF9F6` for content sections
- **Secondary accent**: Sage green `#4A7C59`
- **Headings**: Playfair Display (elegant, evokes fine dining + cultural richness)
- **Body**: Inter (clean, readable)
- **Photography style**: Rich, moody food photography; warm ambient restaurant shots

## Functional Requirements
- **Online ordering** via SpotOn integration (external link: existing client account)
- **Mobile-first** responsive design
- **Fast page loads** — ISR/SSG for all marketing pages
- **SEO** — structured metadata, Open Graph, schema.org Restaurant markup
- **Draft preview** — editors can preview unpublished content in the Next.js frontend

## SEO Requirements
- Title template: `{Page Title} | Thai Nigiri — Sandpoint, ID`
- Meta descriptions per page
- schema.org `Restaurant` type on home page
- Open Graph images per page
- Sitemap.xml generated by Next.js

## Integrations
- **Online ordering**: SpotOn (https://www.spoton.com/order/) — external link, no deep integration needed for v1
- **Analytics**: Google Analytics 4 (tag to be provided by client)
- **Social**: Facebook @thainigirisandpoint

## Editorial Rules
- Menu items managed exclusively in WordPress — editors update price, description, featured flag
- Hero tagline and CTAs managed as page options in WordPress
- About page content fully editorial
- Layout and section structure is code-controlled (no page builder)

## Success Criteria
- [ ] Live preview URL available for client review
- [ ] Full menu visible and organized by category
- [ ] Order Online button prominently placed
- [ ] Hours and location immediately findable
- [ ] Site loads in < 2s on mobile (Lighthouse performance > 90)
- [ ] Client can update menu items without developer involvement
